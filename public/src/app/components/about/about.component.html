What I do

I connect people to brands and organizations by building intuitive, user-centric websites that engage, delight, and communicate clearly on all devices.

How the work is done

We’ll sit down over a coffee and talk through your online objectives to find a solution that works synergistically to drive results.

Goal Identification

Maybe you know exactly what you need or maybe you have no idea what you want. Don't worry, I've developed a step-by-step questionaire that will not only bring a clear picture of your desired website into focus, it will also help me determine the site's unique features.

#1

Planning

This is the fun part. Once we have a vision of where to take your website, I will put together a concept that not only looks great, but it will also translate your hopes for the site into reality. After you've shared your feedback with us, we'll then merge your suggestions into a final design.

#2

Designing

During the design phase, a website takes shape. All the visual content, such as images, photos, and videos is created at this step. In this phase, communication between both you and me is crucial to ensure that the final website will match your needs and taste.

#3

Content Writing

Content writing and compiling usually overlaps with other stages of website creation, and its role can’t be underestimated. At this step it is necessary to put in writing the very essence you’d like to communicate to the audience of your website, and add calls-to-action. Content writing involves also creation of catching headlines, text editing, writing new text, etc.

#4

Development

The developmental stage is the point where the website itself is created. Graphic elements that have been designed during the previous stages should be used to create an actual website.

#5

Testing & Launching

Testing is probably the most routine part of a process. Every single link should be tested to make sure that there are no broken ones among them. Developer should check every form, every script, run a spell-checking software to find possible typos, use code validators to check if the code follows the current web standards. After it is time to upload files to server.
